Mesmerism occupies a distinctive place in the history of Western esotericism, medicine, psychology and symbolic imagination.

Associated with animal magnetism, altered states, invisible forces and the threshold between science and occult philosophy, Mesmerism became one of the great modern examples of how European culture interpreted influence, perception, body, mind and hidden correspondences.

Within Mensur, Mesmerism is approached not as a therapeutic claim, but as a historical and intellectual field: a way of studying the symbolic imagination of Europe, the emergence of psychological modernity, the language of invisible forces and the cultural border between science, ritual and esoteric speculation.

This line belongs to the broader study of symbolism, Western esotericism, intellectual history and the formation of modern European consciousness.
