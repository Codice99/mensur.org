Gabriel Gabrielli Sanchez is a European lawyer, economist and doctoral researcher.

His professional and academic profile combines law, economics, European regulation, financial markets, international trade and institutional analysis.

## Professional profile

He holds the professional qualification of lawyer and is registered as an economist.

His work brings together legal analysis, economic reasoning, regulatory structures, financial markets, international trade and institutional research.

## Education

Gabriel holds a Law Degree from the University of Almeria, a Master’s Degree in International Trade from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, a Master in Financial Markets and Asset Management from Instituto de Estudios Bursatiles, and a Master’s Degree in Access to the Legal Profession from Universidad Autonoma de Madrid.

## Doctoral research

Gabriel is a doctoral researcher in an international academic framework connected with the University of Almeria and the University of Groningen.

His doctoral research focuses on European Union energy law, renewable hydrogen regulation, regulatory design and the development of the European internal energy market.

## Mensur

Mensur is his public intellectual platform and the first expression of the Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project.

The project is devoted to lineage, nobility, aristocratic memory, genealogy, symbolism, Mesmerism, Western esotericism and European intellectual history.

It is conceived as a cultural and intellectual project with a long-term institutional horizon.
