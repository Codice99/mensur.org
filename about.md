Gabriel Gabrielli Sánchez is a European lawyer, economist and doctoral researcher with a multidisciplinary profile across law, economics, European regulation, financial markets, international trade, software engineering, genealogy, nobility, symbolism, Mesmerism, Western esotericism and European intellectual history.

He is the founder of Mensur | A Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project, conceived as a public cultural and intellectual platform devoted to lineage, nobility, aristocratic memory, genealogy, heraldry, symbolic systems, Western esotericism, Mesmerism and European historical consciousness.

## Academic background

Gabriel holds a Law Degree from the University of Almería, completed with an average grade of 8.98/10.

He also holds a Master’s Degree in International Trade from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, completed with an average grade of 8.89/10; a Master in Financial Markets and Asset Management from Instituto de Estudios Bursátiles; and a Master’s Degree in Access to the Legal Profession from Universidad Autónoma de Madrid, completed with an average grade of 7.96/10.

His academic background brings together legal reasoning, economic analysis, financial markets, international trade, regulatory structures and institutional research.

## Doctoral research

Gabriel is a doctoral researcher in an international academic framework connected with the University of Almería and the University of Groningen.

His doctoral research focuses on European Union energy law, renewable hydrogen, the legal requirements for electricity used in the production of renewable hydrogen, regulatory design, market structure and the future development of the European internal energy market.

This doctoral line belongs to his formal academic profile. Mensur, however, is not intended to function as a technical repository for his doctoral work. The project operates as his broader public intellectual platform, focused on genealogy, nobility, symbolism, Western esotericism, Mesmerism and European intellectual history.

## Mensur

Mensur is the public intellectual platform of the Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project.

The name refers to the tradition of Central European academic fencing and evokes measure, discipline, form, honour and continuity. Within this project, Mensur represents a disciplined approach to memory, lineage, symbolism, intellectual history and cultural transmission.

Mensur is not a private archive. It is a public cultural and intellectual project designed to host essays, research notes, bibliographic work, symbolic studies and future institutional development.

## The Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project

The Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project is the institutional horizon of Mensur.

It is intended to develop gradually into a cultural association and, at a later stage, potentially into a foundation or institute dedicated to the study and transmission of European lineage, nobility, aristocratic memory, symbolism, esoteric studies and intellectual history.

Mensur is the first public form of that project.
