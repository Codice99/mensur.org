Gabriel Gabrielli Sánchez is a Spanish and Italian lawyer, economist and doctoral researcher with a multidisciplinary profile across law, economics, European legal thought, software engineering, genealogy, nobility, symbolism, Western esotericism and European intellectual history.

He holds a Law Degree from the University of Almería, a Master’s Degree in International Trade from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, a Master in Financial Markets and Asset Management from Instituto de Estudios Bursátiles, and a Master’s Degree in Access to the Legal Profession from Universidad Autónoma de Madrid.

He also holds the professional qualification of lawyer in Spain and is registered as an economist. His academic and professional background combines legal analysis, economic reasoning, financial markets, international trade, regulatory thinking and institutional research.

## Doctoral and academic profile

Gabriel is a doctoral researcher in an international academic framework connected with the University of Almería and the University of Groningen.

His doctoral trajectory is linked to European legal and regulatory research, comparative legal analysis and the study of institutional design within the European context. Mensur does not function as a technical doctoral repository, but as his broader public intellectual platform.

His academic profile is complemented by studies in software engineering and computer systems, reflecting a wider interest in digital structures, systems thinking, technical knowledge and the intersection between law, institutions and technology.

## Mensur

Mensur is his public intellectual platform and the first expression of the Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project.

The project brings together lineage, nobility, Jewish aristocratic memory, genealogy, heraldry, symbolic systems, Mesmerism, Western esotericism, European history and questions of identity and continuity.

Mensur is conceived not as a private archive, but as a public cultural and intellectual project: a platform for essays, notes, research lines, bibliographic work and future institutional development.

## The Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project

The Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project is the institutional horizon of Mensur.

It is intended to develop gradually into a cultural association and, at a later stage, potentially into a foundation or institute dedicated to the study and transmission of European lineage, nobility, Jewish memory, symbolism, esoteric studies and intellectual history.

Mensur is the first public form of that project.
