Gabriel Gabrielli Sanchez is a European lawyer, economist and doctoral researcher.

His work brings together law, economics, European regulation, institutional analysis, financial markets, international trade and legal research. Alongside his professional and academic profile, he develops Mensur as a public cultural and intellectual platform devoted to lineage, nobility, aristocratic memory, genealogy, symbolism, Mesmerism, Western esotericism and European intellectual history.

## Professional and academic profile

Gabriel holds a Law Degree from the University of Almeria, a Master’s Degree in International Trade from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, a Master in Financial Markets and Asset Management from Instituto de Estudios Bursatiles, and a Master’s Degree in Access to the Legal Profession from Universidad Autonoma de Madrid.

He holds the professional qualification of lawyer in Spain and is registered as an economist.

Gabriel is a doctoral researcher in an international academic framework connected with the University of Almeria and the University of Groningen. His doctoral research focuses on European Union energy law, renewable hydrogen regulation, regulatory design and the development of the European internal energy market.

He is also developing a technical profile through studies in software engineering and computer systems, with an interest in digital structures, systems thinking and the relationship between law, technology and institutions.

## Areas of interest

His areas of work and research include European legal thought, regulatory systems, energy law, institutional design, economics, financial markets, international trade, genealogy, nobility, aristocratic memory, symbolism, Mesmerism, Western esotericism and European intellectual history.

## Mensur

Mensur is the public intellectual platform of the Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project.

The name refers to the tradition of Central European academic fencing and evokes measure, discipline, form, honour and continuity. Within this project, Mensur represents a disciplined approach to memory, lineage, symbolism, intellectual history and cultural transmission.

Mensur is not a technical repository for his doctoral research. It is a broader public cultural and intellectual project designed to host essays, research notes, bibliographic work, symbolic studies and future institutional development.

## Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project

The Weissmann–Greiffenberg Institute Project is the institutional horizon of Mensur.

It is intended to develop gradually into a cultural association and, at a later stage, potentially into a foundation or institute dedicated to the study and transmission of European lineage, nobility, aristocratic memory, symbolism, esoteric studies and intellectual history.

Mensur is the first public form of that project.
